Diriyah

The birthplace of the Saudi state, located on the outskirts of Riyadh. Now a restored cultural and heritage destination featuring mud-brick palaces, traditional architecture, and open-air museums.

Diriyah's History

Located on the northwestern outskirts of the Saudi capital, Riyadh, Diriyah was the original home of the House of Saud, and served as the capital of the Emirate of Diriyah under the first Saudi dynasty from 1727 to 1818.[5] Today, the town is the seat of the Diriyah Governorate, which also includes the villages of Uyayna, Jubayla, and Al-Ammariyyah, among others—and is part of Riyadh Province.
