A vast open-air museum in northwest Saudi Arabia, home to Hegra — the Kingdom's first UNESCO World Heritage Site. Marvel at ancient Nabataean tombs carved into rose-red rock.

HERITAGE

Al-Ula's History

AlUla, located in northwestern Saudi Arabia, is a 200,000-year-old oasis and, for millennia, a vital crossroads of the Incense Route connecting Arabia, Egypt, and India. It served as the capital of the ancient Dadan and Lihyan kingdoms (7th–2nd century BCE) before becoming the southern capital of the Nabataean civilization (1st century BCE) at Hegra, now a UNESCO World Heritage site.
