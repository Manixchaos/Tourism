VISIT SAUDI ARABIA

DESTINATIONS

Saudi Arabia's geography spans vast deserts, mountain ranges, and over 2,600 km of Red Sea coastline. Each region offers a completely distinct experience for travelers.

Medain Saleh (Hegra)

Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site. Over 100 monumental Nabataean tombs with elaborate facades dating back more than 2,000 years, set against dramatic sandstone formations.

Diriyah

The birthplace of the Saudi state, located on the outskirts of Riyadh. Now a restored cultural and heritage destination featuring mud-brick palaces, traditional architecture, and open-air museums.

Al-Ahsa Oasis

Another UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's largest oasis. Famous for its date palms, hot springs, historical forts, and traditional crafts that have thrived for millennia.

NEOM & The Line

A glimpse into the future — NEOM is Saudi Arabia's visionary megaproject on the Red Sea coast, featuring futuristic cities, luxury resorts, and the world's most ambitious urban design.

Farasan Islands

An archipelago of coral islands in the southern Red Sea. Rich in marine biodiversity, ancient ruins, and mangroves — perfect for eco-tourism, snorkeling, and birdwatching.

Hail Region

Home to the Jubbah and Shuwaymis rock art sites — ancient petroglyphs inscribed by early humans and listed as UNESCO World Heritage. A must-see for history enthusiasts.
