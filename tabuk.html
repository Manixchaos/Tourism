Tabuk Region's History

Tabuk in northwestern Saudi Arabia is a historic city with roots dating to 500 BCE, serving as an ancient trade hub and capital for the Lihyanites. It gained prominence as the site of the 630 CE Battle of Tabuk, served as a key Ottoman Hejaz Railway station (1694), and is known for its pre-Islamic springs and deep Islamic heritage.
