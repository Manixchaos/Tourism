Al-Ahsa Oasis

Another UNESCO World Heritage Site and the world's largest oasis. Famous for its date palms, hot springs, historical forts, and traditional crafts that have thrived for millennia.

Al-Ahsa Oasis' History

Al-Ahsa Oasis, located in Saudi Arabia's Eastern Province, is the world's largest self-contained palm oasis, with human habitation dating back to 6000 B.C. due to natural, abundant water springs. Historically a major Neolithic site, it later served as a vital agricultural, trading, and pilgrimage hub influenced by Dilmunite, Islamic, and Ottoman civilizations before becoming part of Saudi Arabia.
