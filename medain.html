Medain Salah(Hegra)

Saudi Arabia's first UNESCO World Heritage Site. Over 100 monumental Nabataean tombs with elaborate facades dating back more than 2,000 years, set against dramatic sandstone formations.

Medain Salah's History

Madain Saleh (also known as Hegra or Al-Hijr) is a major archaeological site in Al-Ula, Saudi Arabia, renowned for 111 monumental tombs carved into sandstone outcrops, largely from the Nabataean Kingdom (1st century BCE – 1st century CE). As the southern capital of the Nabataeans after Petra, it was a vital trade hub on the incense route.
