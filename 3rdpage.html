VISIT SAUDI ARABIA

CULTURE & FOOD

Saudi culture is rich, warm, and steeped in ancient traditions. Hospitality is a cornerstone of Saudi life — guests are always welcomed with dates and Arabic coffee (qahwa).

TRADITIONAL DISHES

Kabsa

The national dish. Fragrant basmati rice cooked with meat (lamb or chicken), tomatoes, and a distinctive spice blend including cardamom, cloves, and saffron.

MAIN DISH

Mandi

Slow-cooked meat and rice prepared in a tandoor oven underground. The result is incredibly tender, smoky, and aromatic — a feast for any occasion.

MAIN DISH

Jareesh

A hearty porridge of crushed wheat cooked with meat or chicken, onions, and spices. A traditional dish enjoyed especially during Ramadan and celebrations.

TRADITIONAL

Cultural Etiquette

Dress modestly in public spaces. Greet with "As-salamu alaykum." Accept coffee and dates when offered — it is a sign of hospitality. Photography of people should always be done with permission.

Festivals & Events

The Riyadh Season and Jeddah Season are major entertainment festivals held annually. AlUla hosts world-class arts events. The Saudi National Day on September 23 features celebrations across the Kingdom.
