Mandi

Slow-cooked meat and rice prepared in a tandoor oven underground. The result is incredibly tender, smoky, and aromatic — a feast for any occasion.

About Mandi

Mandi is an Arab traditional dish that originated from the Hadhramaut region in Yemen. It consists mainly of meat and rice with a blend of spices, and is cooked in a pit. It is consumed in most areas of the Arabian Peninsula and also found in Egypt, Hyderabad Deccan, the Levant, Turkey, Kerala and Southeast Asia.
